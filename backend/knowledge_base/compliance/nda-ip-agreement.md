# NDA and Intellectual Property Agreement Guidelines

As an employee joining our engineering and product development teams, you will be granted access to classified proprietary information, architectural blueprints, ongoing strategy memos, and unsanitized datasets. This document summarizes the binding legal commitments articulated in your formal Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) and Intellectual Property (IP) Assignment documentation.

## Scope of Confidential Information
Confidential information is broadly defined as any data, strategies, codebases, or communications you engage with internally that are not available to the general public.
This includes, but is not limited to:
- Source code, algorithms, and infrastructure definitions.
- Customer lists, financial ledgers, and marketing strategies.
- Unreleased product features, roadmaps, and UI/UX mockups.
- Passwords, internal API keys, and deployment procedures.

You are legally bound not to disclose, share, blog post, screenshot, or externally discuss any confidential information to unauthorized third parties without explicit legal clearance.

## Intellectual Property (IP) Assignment Policy
The software industry heavily relies on clear ownership boundaries concerning Intellectual Property. To protect the company's investments and commercial interests, an IP assignment policy is enforced across all employment tiers.
- **Corporate Ownership:** Any code, invention, design, algorithm, or document you create during company working hours, or while utilizing any company-provided equipment (such as your corporate laptop), or leveraging company datasets, strictly and entirely belongs to the company.
- **Open Source Contributions:** We encourage supporting the open source community. However, if you wish to contribute to an external open source project using company hardware, or if you intend to open-source a utility script you built for work, you must clear this request through the Open Source Review Board (OSRB).
- **Personal Projects:** Software you build entirely on your own time, disconnected from the corporate network, utilizing exclusively personal hardware, and which does not compete directly with our market strategies, belongs to you.

## Offboarding and Contract Termination
If your employment concludes, the non-disclosure aspects of your NDA remain fully active. You are perpetually prohibited from utilizing acquired trade secrets at future employers. You must immediately surrender all hardware, access keys, codebase clones, and physical documentation upon termination.

## HR Signing Procedure
You must formally execute these documents before receiving Git, AWS, or production database permissions.
1. Upon your first login, a specialized secure DocuSign link will be dispatched to your corporate email from **hr@company.com**.
2. Thoroughly read the entire master contract. 
3. Execute the e-signature packet. The HR portal will automatically process the signature and ping our Identity Access Management (IAM) system to unlock your developer accounts. If your link expires, contact the HR helpdesk directly.
